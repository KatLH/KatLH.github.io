Commonly Used Terms

Below is a short list of commonly used terms and acronyms dealing with waste management and reduction.


  • Compost

    Organic matter that has been decomposed in a process called composting, which produces a nutrient rich soil conditioner.

  • EPA

    Environmental Protection Agency

  • Municipal Solid Waste (MSW)

    Municipal Solid Waste (MSW) is commonly known as trash and refers to various items consumers throw away after they are used.

  • Recycling

    Recycling is the process of converting waste into reusable material.

  • Sustainable Materials Management (SMM)

    Sustainable Materials Management (SMM) is the use and reuse of materials across their entire lifecycle.

  • Waste Generation

    Waste generation refers to the weight or volume of materials and products that enter the waste stream before recycling, composting, landfilling, or combustion takes place.

  • Waste Reduction Model (WARM)

    The Waste Reduction Model (WARM) is a tool for estimating greenhouse gas reductions resulting from SMM.



What is Municipal Solid Waste (MSW)?

In 2015, 262.4 million tons of MSW generated.

MSW is:

  1. Bottles
  2. Corrugated boxes
  3. Food
  4. Grass clippings
  5. Sofa
  6. Computers
  7. Tires
  8. Refrigerators

MSW is NOT:

  1. Construction and demolition debris
  2. Municipal wastewater sludge
  3. non-hazardous industrial wastes
Waste Fact:
Paper and paperboard are the largest material component of MSW.

What is Sustainable Materials Management (SMM)?

The EPA has transitioned their focus from waste management to waste reduction and reuse.

In 2015, 1.56 lbs of MSW was recycled per person per day.

91 million tons of the 262.4 million tons of MSW generated in 2015 was made of recycled and composted materials.

67.7 million tons came from recycled materials

2.1 million tons came from food

21.3 million tons came from yard trimmings

1.2 million tons came from consumer electronics
